The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our Solar System. It is located approximately 93 million miles from our planet and is the most important source of energy for life on Earth.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and is the smallest planet within the solar system. It is located approximately 36 million miles from the Sun and features a rocky surface.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is about the same size as Earth and is around 67 million miles from the sun. Venus is considered to be the hottest planet in the solar system.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and is the planet we all live on! Earth is the only known planet with life on it and has a surface of 70% ocean and 30% rock.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun. It is located about 155 million miles from the Sun and its volume is 15% that of Earth's. For a long time it was rumored to have aliens living on it.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun. It is the largest of all the planets and is located about 460 million miles from the Sun. It is the first planet to be considered a gas giant.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun. Also a gas giant, Saturn is the second largest planet and is located about 909 million miles from the Sun. Saturn is most well known for its rings.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun at a distance of about 1.8 billion miles. It is the third biggest planet and features a beautiful blue hue due to its methane atmosphere.

Neptune

Neptune is the eigth and final planet from the sun. Holding the spot of fourth biggest planet, it is located about 2.8 billion miles from the Sun and takes 165 Earth years to complete one orbit.
